# Percentage Calculator

## What it does
The Percentage Calculator is a versatile math utility designed to solve the most common percentage-based problems. It features three distinct modes:
1.  **Finding a percentage of a number:** (e.g., "What is 15% of 200?")
2.  **Finding the percentage relationship:** (e.g., "50 is what percent of 200?")
3.  **Calculating percentage change:** (e.g., "What is the percentage increase from 100 to 150?")

## Why someone uses it
-   **Business & Finance:** For calculating tax, tips, discounts, and profit margins.
-   **Data Analysis:** To determine growth rates, performance metrics, and statistical distributions.
-   **Daily Life:** Calculating savings, interest rates, or adjusting recipes.
-   **Speed and Accuracy:** To get instant results without having to remember formulas or use a complex scientific calculator.

## Step-by-step instructions
### 1. Calculate a Percentage of a Value
-   Navigate to the first card: **"What is [X] % of [Y]"**.
-   Enter the percentage in the first box and the total number in the second box.
-   The result appears instantly in the third box.

### 2. Calculate Percentage of Two Numbers
-   Navigate to the second card: **"[X] is what percent of [Y]"**.
-   Enter the partial value in the first box and the total value in the second box.
-   The result (the percentage) will be shown in the final box.

### 3. Calculate Percentage Increase or Decrease
-   Navigate to the third card: **"What is the percentage increase/decrease"**.
-   Enter your starting value in the **"From"** box.
-   Enter your final value in the **"To"** box.
-   The percentage change will be displayed instantly. A negative result indicates a decrease.

## Examples
-   **Shopping:** You want to find 20% off a $85 item. Enter 20 and 85 in the first calculator. Result: $17.
-   **Test Scores:** You got 18 out of 24 questions right. Enter 18 and 24 in the second calculator. Result: 75%.
-   **Investment Growth:** Your stock went from $120 to $150. Enter 120 and 150 in the third calculator. Result: 25% increase.

## FAQs
-   **How do I interpret a negative result in the increase/decrease calculator?** A negative result means there was a percentage decrease. For example, going from 100 to 80 will result in -20%.
-   **Does the tool handle decimals?** Yes, you can enter decimal numbers in any of the fields for precise calculations.
-   **What happens if I divide by zero?** If you enter 0 in a denominator field (like the "Y" in "X is what percent of Y"), the tool will return an empty result as division by zero is mathematically undefined.
-   **Is the result rounded?** The tool provides high-precision results suitable for most technical and financial applications.

## Common mistakes
-   **Confusing "From/To" order:** In the increase/decrease calculator, swapping the "From" and "To" values will give you the wrong perspective on the change.
-   **Percentage as Decimal:** Entering `0.05` in the percentage field when you mean `5%`. The tool expects the percentage as a whole number.
-   **Swapping X and Y:** In the "X is what percent of Y" calculator, make sure the "part" goes in the first box and the "whole" in the second.
