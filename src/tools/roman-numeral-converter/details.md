# Roman Numeral Converter

## What it does
The Roman Numeral Converter is a bidirectional tool that translates Arabic (decimal) numbers into Roman numerals and vice versa. It follows the standard subtractive notation (e.g., 4 is `IV`, not `IIII`) and supports values ranging from 1 to 3,999. It provides real-time validation to ensure that any Roman numeral entered is mathematically valid.

## Why someone uses it
- **Historical Research:** Decoding dates on monuments, old books, or movie credits.
- **Academic Writing:** Correctly numbering outlines, prefaces, or appendices in formal documents.
- **Design & Aesthetics:** Finding the Roman equivalent for clock faces, tattoos, or jewelry engravings.
- **Pop Culture:** Determining the edition number for major events like the Super Bowl or the Olympics.

## Step-by-step instructions
### Convert Arabic to Roman
1. Navigate to the **Arabic to Roman** card.
2. Enter a whole number between **1 and 3,999**.
3. The Roman numeral equivalent will appear instantly in the result area.
4. Click **Copy** to save the result.

### Convert Roman to Arabic
1. Navigate to the **Roman to Arabic** card.
2. Type or paste a Roman numeral (e.g., `MCMXCIV`).
3. The decimal value will be displayed instantly.
4. If the input is invalid, an error message will appear, and the result will be hidden.
5. Click **Copy** to save the result.

## Examples
- **Arabic 2024:** Results in `MMXXIV`.
- **Arabic 99:** Results in `XCIX`.
- **Roman MCMLXXXVIII:** Results in `1988`.
- **Roman CDXLIV:** Results in `444`.

## FAQs
- **What is the maximum number I can convert?** The standard Roman numeral system used here supports numbers up to **3,999**. Values above this require special symbols (macrons) that are not part of the standard ASCII set.
- **Does it support zero?** No. The ancient Romans did not have a symbol for zero.
- **Can I use lowercase letters?** Yes, the tool accepts both uppercase and lowercase Roman numerals.
- **Why is 4 not IIII?** While some clock faces use `IIII`, the standard modern convention is the "subtractive" rule where `IV` (5-1) is used for 4 and `IX` (10-1) is used for 9.

## Common mistakes
- **Invalid Sequences:** Entering characters that don't follow the subtractive rules (e.g., `IL` for 49 instead of `XLIX`).
- **Exceeding the Limit:** Trying to convert numbers higher than 3,999 or lower than 1.
- **Mixing Symbols:** Including non-Roman characters like `A`, `B`, or `S` in the input field.
